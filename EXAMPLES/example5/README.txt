***Purpose of the test campaign***
The experiment was set up into two complementing experiment, which was the incubation experiment and a pot experiment. The purpose of both experiments
was to investigate the N mineralization rates of organic fertilizers compost and bokashi made using aquatic weeds water hyacinth and hippo grass.
The pot experiment included a tropical soil treated with the different organic amendments, and sorghum plants were planted. Complementing the pot experiment,
an incubation experiment was set up with the same soil, same rate of amendments, but without any plants. The incubation experiment was set up to understand what 
was happening in the pot experiment, excluding the plants. The purpose of the incubation experiment was to determine the N mineralization rates of the organic amendments.

***Test Equipment***
The pH of the samples was determined using a Hanna HI 8424 pH meter. The exchangeable bases and micro elements were measured using a perkin Elmer AAnalyst 400 Atomic
Absorption Spectrometer. Total Phosphorous was determined using a Jenway 6305 UV-visible Spectrophotometer at a wavelength of 882 nm.

***Description of the data in the data set***
The data included in the data set has been organized based on different sets of experiments and amendments. The amendment characterization contains parameters of the 
organic amendments, and these parameter are sorted per column. The mineralization data contains total sol mineral N after being treated with the differetn organic amendments.
The Sorghum data includes parameters for the sorghum plants from the pot experiment. The data set also cotains columns of soil characteristics and application rates of organic 
amendments.

***Data specific information***
Abberviations and full names of the amendments include: WHC = water hyacinth compost, WHB = Water hyacinth bokashi, HGC = Hippo grass compost, HGB = Hippo grass bokashi
Fert = inorganic fertilizer, Con = Control, HG = Hippo grass, WH = Water hyacinth.
